United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

The Security Council is one of the oldest and most important council in the UN. It has the primary responsibility for the maintenance of international peace and security. It has 15 Members, and each Member has one vote. Under the Charter, all Member States are obligated to comply with Council decisions. A small committee, discussions are often on matters critical to the maintenance of peace and accord among countries.
